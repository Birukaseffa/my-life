B,Vacuum Tube

a vacuum tube , also known as an electron tube or just a tube, is a device that controls electric current flow in a high vacuum between electrodes to create light in the form of cathode ray tubes, or to control electronic signals in various electronic devices. it was used in early electronic devices such as radios,televisions, and computers,before the invention of the transistor.

A vacuum tube is a device used to control the flow of electric current using a vacuum in a sealed container, which usually takes the form of a glass tube, hence the name. The vacuum tube is the predecessor of the modern transistor and was used in similar ways as electronically controlled switches, rectifiers, amplifiers, oscillators and in other creative ways that transistors may be used today. The cathode ray tube (CRT) used extensively in early television sets and computer monitors as screens is a kind of vacuum tube.

a vacuum tube cocnsists of a sealed glass or metal envelope that contains a cathode,an anode,and one or more electrodes. the cathode is heated to release electrons, which are then controlled by the electrodes and directed towards the anode,creating a flow of current.the vacuum inside the tube allows for high level of control over over the electrons,making it possible to amplify signals,switch signals,and perform other functions in early electronic devices.

D,ARM Architecture

ARM is a type of instruction set architecture (ISA) that is used in the design of microprocessors and other types of processors. it stands for Advanced RISC Machine and is a reduced instruction set computing(RISC) architecture.

It was developed in 1980s and has since become one of the most widely used architecture in the world.it used in a variety of devices,including smartphones,tables,and other embedded system, due to their low power consumption and high performace.

The ARM architecture is defined by a set of specifications that outline the instruction set and register set of the processor, as well as the memory architecture and input/output(I/O)interfaces. ARM processors are available in various different versions,known as "architecture,"which are designated by numbers such as ARMv6,ARMv7,and ARMv8.
